Canned Fish

Spices and Seasonings

Ingredients:

  • 3 c anchovy broth
  • 1/2 c prepared kimchi
  • 1 Tbsp gochujaru
  • 1 Tbsp soya sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 clove minced garlic
  • 1 block tofu, sliced
  • 1 handful of mung bean noodles
  • 1 can tuna
  • 2 green onions

Instructions:

  1. Bloom your garlic and gochujaru in sesame oil, stirring frequently. I used olive oil and added the sesame once the pan was hot and garlic frying.
  2. Add your kimchi and stir-fry.
  3. Prepare your anchovy broth and bring to a boil. Add more water as needed to ensure the ingredients are covered. Stir in soya sauce. I added some pepper paste.
  4. Add drained canned tuna, tofu, and noodles. I like the texture of mung bean noodles. I added winterbor kale because that was the vegetable I had.
  5. Serve, preferably seated on the ground and eat from the pot using a ladle/spoon/chopstick combination, like hot pot. Garnish with green onions.

Reference:

Anchovy broth is a star in my cooking and I love that the whole, dried fish are placed in the packets. I received suggestions for a variety of jjigae and kalguksu dishes when developing this meal. I opted for a combination, which I learned is actually already a Korean dish per Maangchi. I don't have a stone pot but my stock pot works just fine.
